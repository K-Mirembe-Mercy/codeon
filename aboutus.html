OUR STORY

How CODEON Was Born

From a small idea in Kampala to Uganda's leading student coding platform.

💡
2023 · THE IDEA

A Problem Worth Solving

A group of students in Kampala realized that Ugandan learners were graduating without the practical skills employers wanted. Most coding courses taught theory — not GitHub, not deployment, not real collaboration. CODEON was the answer.

🚀
2023 · LAUNCH

First Students, First Lessons

CODEON launched with its first cohort of HTML and CSS students. The feedback was immediate — students wanted more structure, more competition, and more community. So we built it.

🏆
2024 · HACKATHON

The First National Hackathon

CODEON hosted its first national hackathon, bringing together students from across Uganda to compete in real web development challenges. The energy was electric — and it proved that Ugandan students thrive under real competitive pressure.

📊
2024 · GROWTH

500+ Students & Counting

CODEON crossed 500 active students, with learners from Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania, and Nigeria. The community platform launched with daily challenges, leaderboards, and group chat — making learning social and addictive.

🌍
2025 · TODAY

Building Africa's Developer Generation

Today CODEON operates a full platform — daily challenges in 3 languages, weekly championships, verified certificates, and a growing mentorship program. The mission is unchanged: build real developers, rooted in Africa.

⚠️ Firebase Quota Full — How to Fix This

If your Firebase project is showing quota errors or "authentication failed", it usually means one of three things. Here is exactly how to fix each one:

  1. Firestore free quota exceeded (most common): Go to Firebase Console → your project → Firestore Database → Usage. If the reads/writes are at the free limit (50,000 reads/day, 20,000 writes/day), you have two options: upgrade to the Blaze pay-as-you-go plan (very cheap for small traffic), or wait 24 hours for quota to reset.
  2. Authentication not enabled: Go to Firebase Console → Authentication → Sign-in method → Enable Email/Password → Save. Without this step, all logins fail with an auth error.
  3. Missing API key fields: Your firebaseConfig in the HTML must include ALL fields: apiKey, authDomain, projectId, storageBucket, messagingSenderId, and appId. Go to Firebase Console → Project Settings → Your Apps → copy the complete config.
  4. Firestore security rules blocking requests: Go to Firestore Database → Rules tab and make sure the rules allow authenticated reads/writes. For development, you can temporarily use: allow read, write: if request.auth != null;
  5. Upgrade tip: The Firebase Blaze plan charges by actual usage. For a student platform with under 1,000 users, the monthly cost is typically $0–$2 USD. Upgrading removes all quota limits.

To upgrade: Firebase Console → top-left dropdown → Upgrade plan → choose Blaze → add a payment card. You will only be charged if you exceed free tier limits.
